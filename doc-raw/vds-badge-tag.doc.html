Usage patterns & best practices

Keep badge labels short, attach them to content they describe, and avoid stacking multiple badge types on the same surface.

Do

  • Use semantic colors for status or workflow signals.
  • Keep labels to one or two words and align with the data it labels.
  • Use dot badges for quick state scanning in lists or tables.
  • Use tags for filters or categories that users can act on.
  • Show counts only when the number drives urgency or attention.

Do not

  • Do not stack multiple badges to communicate a single status.
  • Do not rely on color alone; pair with text or dot indicators.
  • Do not use badges as primary navigation or CTA elements.
  • Do not use sharp corners unless the system uses sharp tags consistently.
  • Do not overload cards with too many badge styles at once.

Accessibility & usage rules

Keep badges readable, provide accessible labels for icon-only tags, and use semantic HTML elements when badges are interactive.

Do

  • Use <button> for interactive tags and filters.
  • Provide aria-label for icon-only badges or remove controls.
  • Ensure text has sufficient contrast for the chosen variant.
  • Use badge--outline when the surface is highly saturated.

Do not

  • Do not hide status behind color-only badges without text.
  • Do not use badges as the only focusable element in a row.
  • Do not nest multiple interactive controls inside a badge.
  • Do not use small badges (badge--xs) for long labels.
