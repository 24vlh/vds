Usage patterns & best practices

Avatars should clarify identity, ownership, or presence without distracting from content.

Do

  • Use images when available and initials as fallbacks.
  • Keep avatar sizes consistent within the same surface.
  • Use groups for shared ownership or participant lists.
  • Use color variants sparingly to indicate teams or roles.
  • Keep status dots consistent across the product.

Do not

  • Do not mix multiple sizes in the same row.
  • Do not use avatars as primary navigation elements.
  • Do not place labels inside avatars; keep text outside.
  • Do not rely on color alone for identity or role.
  • Do not stack too many avatars without a count indicator.

Accessibility & usage rules

Provide accessible text for image avatars, keep contrast sufficient, and avoid using avatars as the only status indicator.

Do

  • Use alt text for images that convey identity.
  • Use aria-label when avatars are interactive.
  • Keep initials to two characters for readability.
  • Pair status dots with a text label in the UI.

Do not

  • Do not leave image alt attributes empty when needed.
  • Do not use low-contrast color combinations for initials.
  • Do not animate status dots excessively.
  • Do not hide critical status only inside the avatar.
