Android-first screens

Android Shell

A fuller Android app shell for dashboards, inboxes, alerts, settings, and adaptive workspaces. It combines safe-area layout, clearer navigation, and shell-specific helpers for stats, filters, banners, push messages, and timelines.

The shell follows the Android guidance mirrored in @24vlh/agents/docs_md/mobile-design: protect content from system bars, keep primary actions reachable, keep destination counts focused, and prefer strong readability over decorative color.

  • Safe areas: top and bottom padding respect device insets.
  • Navigation: bottom nav stays readable and is designed for three to five peer destinations.
  • Shell helpers: dashboards, filters, banners, notices, chips, and timelines are included for common app states.
  • Adaptive layout: panes expand with container queries instead of layout hacks.

Usage and accessibility rules

Android-style shells must feel practical and calm. Readability, reachability, and clear hierarchy matter more than aggressive branding inside app chrome.

Do

  • Keep bottom navigation readable and reserve it for three to five peer destinations.
  • Use hero, banner, and stat helpers to explain state before the user starts reading rows.
  • Label filter groups clearly and keep active filter states readable without flooding the surface with accent color.
  • Group settings and activity into smaller surfaces with short supporting text.
  • Move detail, diagnostics, and controls into a supporting pane when more width becomes available.
  • Reinforce urgency with labels, badges, and copy, not with color alone.

Do not

  • Do not use the accent color as the main reading color for large navigation states.
  • Do not confuse screen-local filters with primary navigation destinations.
  • Do not stack multiple equally-loud banners, heroes, and FABs on the same screen.
  • Do not stretch one endless column on wider layouts when a second pane would read better.
  • Do not overload the top bar with many equal-priority actions.
  • Do not bury critical controls under gesture or system-bar areas.

Accessibility

  • Keep interactive targets at or above 48px. The shell defaults honor that floor.
  • Expose the current destination with aria-current="page" in bottom navigation.
  • Provide labels for icon-only controls and keep focus rings visible.
  • Use aria-pressed for toggle-like filter chips and promote a filter group to a toolbar only when keyboard focus management is implemented.
  • Keep text/icon contrast strong enough to meet Android accessibility guidance.

Pair with other VDS layers

  • Use buttons.css for top-bar, hero, and footer actions.
  • Use forms.css / forms-advanced.css for toggles and grouped settings controls.
  • Use tabs.css when a shell also needs secondary sibling navigation inside a destination.
  • Use overlays.css or toasts.css when the app needs modal or transient layers above the shell.
