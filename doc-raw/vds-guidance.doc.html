Guidance Blocks

Guidance blocks help users scan, understand intent, and act with confidence. Use them to explain what is expected, why it matters, what to avoid, and how to move forward without adding visual weight.

Source: guidance.css - Depends on primitives.css, themes/graphite.css, base.css, layout.css, typography.css, icons.css.

Guidance blocks are not alerts. They are calm, structured explanations that clarify intent and improve task accuracy. Keep them short, structured, and paired with the area they explain.

Overview and dependencies

Guidance blocks are fully token-driven and theme-aware. They rely on spacing, border, radius, and semantic color tokens. No JS required.

  • Use guidance to explain intent, not to report system state.
  • Prefer short lists and one sentence body text.
  • Pair guidance with form sections or decision points.
  • Use semantic variants sparingly and consistently.
  • Keep icons consistent with the tone of the block.

Guidance panels

The core guidance panel supports do, do not, how, why, expect, and recommend messaging. Use short lists for scan-ability.

Do
High impact actions
  • Lead with a one sentence hook.
  • Keep beats to 3 or 6 steps.
  • State the decision the viewer must make.
Outcome Clear promise, faster comprehension.
Do not
Accuracy killers
  • Do not explain every edge case.
  • Avoid switching topics mid section.
  • Do not hide the decision behind context.
Why
Reasoning
A clear promise builds trust and keeps the viewer focused on the decision.
Expect
Completion criteria
You should have a draft title, hook, and beats before moving to video draft.
Recommended
Optional but strong
Keep the notes short. Long notes belong in a separate document.

    </span>
    <div class="guidance__title">Do</div>
  </div>
  <ul class="guidance__list">
    <li>Lead with a one sentence hook.</li>
  </ul>
</div>

Form guidance blocks

Pair guidance with a field to explain intent, quality bars, and what to avoid. Use top, bottom, or wrapped layouts depending on how critical the instruction is.

Hook
Promise in one line
State the payoff in 8 to 12 words. Lead with a verb.
  • One outcome
  • Verb first
  • No qualifiers
Required
Keep it under 12 words.
Optional
Use bullets or short sentences.
What not to do
Avoid production notes
Save editing and publishing steps for later stages.
  • No script paragraphs
  • No camera setup details
  • No timestamps
Brainstorming
Capture intent first
Write the thesis and beats as short, teachable actions.
Required
Use 3 to 6 beats, one action each.
Quality bar
Each beat should be teachable
If a beat needs context, split it into two beats.

Usage rules

Keep guidance blocks direct and calm. Pair them with the exact section they explain.

Do

  • Wrap guidance in [data-vds-guidance] or .vds-guidance.
  • Use guidance for intent, constraints, or expected output.
  • Keep titles short and body text under two lines.
  • Prefer lists over paragraphs in dense forms.
  • Use data-variant only when meaning changes.
  • Place guidance next to the form section it describes.

Do not

  • Do not use guidance for error or system state.
  • Do not stack multiple long guidance blocks together.
  • Avoid vague instructions without concrete examples.
  • Do not hide critical steps inside optional guidance text.
Notes 
1. Keep guidance short: title + one sentence or list.
2. Use data-variant only when meaning changes.
3. Place guidance next to the form section it describes.
4. Avoid using guidance to report errors or system status.
5. Do not overload users with multiple guidance blocks in a row.
