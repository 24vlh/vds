Usage patterns & best practices

Progress indicators should reflect real completion, not fake movement. Match the density of the surface and keep labels specific.

Do

  • Use determinate progress when completion is measurable.
  • Use indeterminate only for short, unknown durations.
  • Label progress with clear, task-specific language.
  • Use stepper states to show completed and active steps.
  • Keep progress bars full width within their container.

Do not

  • Do not animate progress without real progress updates.
  • Do not reset progress to zero without explanation.
  • Do not mix multiple progress styles in one workflow.
  • Do not use steppers for single-step tasks.
  • Do not hide critical progress updates in tooltips only.

Accessibility & usage rules

Expose progress values to assistive tech and ensure visible contrast for the bar, track, and labels.

Do

  • Use aria-valuenow and aria-valuemax for progress bars.
  • Provide text labels when the bar is the only indicator.
  • Respect reduced motion by avoiding fast animations.
  • Use semantic colors that maintain contrast on the track.

Do not

  • Do not rely on color alone to convey completion.
  • Do not use indeterminate progress for long operations.
  • Do not hide step labels when the flow requires context.
  • Do not allow progress bars to shrink below readable sizes.
