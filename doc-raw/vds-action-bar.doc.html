Action bars

Usage patterns & best practices

Action bars provide bulk actions and selection context. Keep the copy concise and the action set focused.

Do

  • Show selected counts and the target noun clearly.
  • Use a single primary action with secondary actions beside it.
  • Provide a clear dismiss or clear selection control.
  • Use sticky bars in long scrolling selections.
  • Use compact bars for dense tables.

Do not

  • Do not include more than 4-5 actions in one bar.
  • Do not hide the dismiss action when selections are active.
  • Do not use action bars for single-item actions.
  • Do not place destructive actions without confirmation.
  • Do not detach the bar from the selected region.

Accessibility & usage rules

Ensure action bars remain keyboard accessible and announce selection changes.

Do

  • Use real <button> elements for actions.
  • Update selection counts in the DOM for screen readers.
  • Keep focus states visible against the bar surface.
  • Provide aria-label for icon-only actions.

Do not

  • Do not trap focus inside the bar unnecessarily.
  • Do not hide actions behind hover-only menus.
  • Do not use low-contrast text for the selection count.
  • Do not remove actions without clearing selection state.
