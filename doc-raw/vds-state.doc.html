Usage patterns & best practices

A good empty state gives context, removes uncertainty, and offers a clear next action.

Do

  • Explain why the section is empty in plain language.
  • Offer one primary action and optional secondary action.
  • Use the split layout for onboarding and setup prompts.
  • Keep copy short and actionable.
  • Show lightweight tips in state__meta when helpful.

Do not

  • Do not overwhelm users with multiple CTAs.
  • Do not hide critical instructions outside the empty state.
  • Do not use empty states to advertise unrelated features.
  • Do not add large illustrations in dense admin views.
  • Do not use empty states as error messaging.

Accessibility & usage rules

Ensure empty states are readable, actionable, and provide semantic context for assistive tech.

Do

  • Use headings for the state title to establish hierarchy.
  • Ensure actions are focusable and keyboard accessible.
  • Keep icon text alternatives clear for screen readers.
  • Provide short helper text for the next steps.

Do not

  • Do not rely on icon-only messages without text.
  • Do not hide core guidance inside tooltips only.
  • Do not use empty states as destructive confirmations.
  • Do not remove the empty state without replacing context.
